## ü§ù **Co-Founder Equity (The Real Numbers)**

### **Standard Equity Splits:**
- **50/50 split** is NOT automatic - it depends on what they bring
- **Technical founder (you)** typically retains more equity since you built everything
- **Realistic ranges:**
  - You: 60-70% | Co-founder: 30-40% (if they bring capital + business skills)
  - You: 70-80% | Co-founder: 20-30% (if they bring only business skills)
  - You: 55-65% | Co-founder: 35-45% (if they bring significant capital)

### **What Those Business Owners Could Bring:**
- **Local studio connections** (massive value for you)
- **Marketing/sales expertise** you lack
- **Business operations** knowledge
- **Capital** for initial marketing spend
- **Credibility** with Vancouver business community

**My Take:** Talk to them, but **don't agree to anything over 40%** unless they're investing significant cash ($50k+).

## üìã **NDAs - When and Why**

### **Studios: YES, Use NDAs**
- **Why:** Protects your commission model and studio partnership strategy
- **When:** Before showing partner portal or discussing revenue splits
- **Template:** Simple 1-page mutual NDA (find templates online)

### **Alpha Testers: NO NDAs Needed**
- They're testing user experience, not business strategy
- Too formal for alpha testing phase
- Focus on getting feedback, not protecting secrets

### **Business Partners: YES, Detailed NDAs**
- Especially if discussing financial projections
- Include non-compete clauses for Vancouver market
- Get lawyer review ($500-1000 investment worth it)

## ü§î **Should You Take on a Co-Founder?**

### **YES, IF they bring:**
- **Studio connections** (can get you 10+ partnerships quickly)
- **Marketing expertise** (proven track record with local businesses)
- **Capital** ($25k+ to fund user acquisition)
- **Operations experience** (can handle customer support, studio relations)

### **NO, IF they only offer:**
- "Business advice" without specific skills
- "Connections" they can't prove
- Wanting equal equity for minimal contribution
- No skin in the game (time or money)

### **Alternative: Advisory Equity**
- Give 2-5% equity for specific help (studio introductions, marketing strategy)
- No voting rights, just financial upside
- Less commitment, easier to manage
