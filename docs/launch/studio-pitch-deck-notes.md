## Deck Presentation Tips

### Before Presenting
1. **Know your numbers cold**: 70% vs 45%, $250 more, 6 months free, $75/month
2. **Practice out loud**: Say the pitch 10 times at home before first visit
3. **Anticipate objections**: Rehearse responses to common questions
4. **Be confident but humble**: You're offering value, not begging

### During Presentation
1. **Make eye contact**: Don't read from slides‚Äîglance and explain
2. **Use hands**: Point to visuals while explaining
3. **Pause for questions**: Don't rush‚Äîlet them absorb
4. **Watch body language**: Crossed arms = skeptical, leaning in = interested
5. **Match their energy**: Enthusiastic owner? Be enthusiastic. Cautious owner? Be measured and detail-oriented.

### After Presenting
1. **Shut up and listen**: After the ask ("Can we schedule 15 minutes?"), STOP TALKING. Let them respond.
2. **Don't oversell**: If they say yes, great! If they say maybe, respect it.
3. **Always leave materials**: Even if they say no, leave one-pager‚Äîthey might change their mind.
4. **Follow up religiously**: Promised email in 24 hours? Send in 23 hours.

## Deck Variations by Studio Type

### **For High-Volume Studios** (e.g., big fitness studio)
- Emphasize: "50-100 intro sessions/month = $4K-9K monthly revenue"
- Emphasize: "We handle all marketing, you handle class delivery"
- Downplay: Feature customization (they care more about volume)

### **For Artisan Studios** (e.g., small pottery studio)
- Emphasize: "Keep your customer relationships‚Äîwe're just the discovery layer"
- Emphasize: "Quality students who genuinely want to learn your craft"
- Emphasize: "Direct input on platform design‚Äîyou shape the product"

### **For Multi-Category Studios** (e.g., arts collective)
- Emphasize: "List all your different class types‚Äîpottery, painting, sculpture"
- Emphasize: "Cross-promotion opportunity‚Äîstudents discover other classes"
- Emphasize: "One platform for your entire schedule"

### **For Skeptical Studios** (e.g., already burned by platforms)
- Emphasize: "Zero upfront cost‚Äîonly pay if it works"
- Emphasize: "I'm a local founder, not a corporation‚Äîyou have my personal cell"
- Emphasize: "Cancel anytime, no penalty‚Äîthis is risk-free"

## Red Flags (When to Walk Away)

Sometimes a studio isn't a good fit. Look for:
- ‚ùå **Hostile attitude**: Rude, dismissive, aggressive (not worth your time)
- ‚ùå **No beginner classes**: Advanced-only studios don't fit our model
- ‚ùå **Unrealistic demands**: "I want 90% revenue share" (economics don't work)
- ‚ùå **Chronically unavailable**: Won't make time, reschedules 3+ times (not serious)
- ‚ùå **Bad online reputation**: <3 star reviews, fraud complaints (brand risk)

**It's okay to say:** "Thanks for your time, but I don't think we're the right fit right now. Best of luck!"

**Why walk away:** 10 great partners > 20 mediocre partners. Quality over quantity.
