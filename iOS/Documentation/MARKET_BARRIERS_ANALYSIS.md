### 1. Market Saturation Reality

#### Current Competitive Landscape
- **15+ active competitors** in Vancouver fitness/hobby booking space

### 2. The "Gym Guilt" Psychology Phenomenon

#### User Abandonment Patterns
```
Day 1-3:   77% abandon (excitement wears off)
Week 1:    40% remaining (reality sets in)
Month 1:   47% churn (habits not formed)
Month 3:   50% total loss (guilt accumulates)
Month 6:   Only 24% active (survivors)
```

#### Psychological Barriers
- **Commitment anxiety**: Fear of another unused subscription
- **Decision fatigue**: Too many class options
- **Imposter syndrome**: "I'm not fit/skilled enough"
- **Social anxiety**: Fear of judgment in classes
- **Financial guilt**: Previous wasted memberships

- **Revenue cannibalization fears**
- **Brand dilution concerns**

#### Studio Pain Points
```
"ClassPass users don't tip"
```

### 4. Credit System Confusion

#### Mental Accounting Challenges
- **Value ambiguity**: "Is 2 credits worth $4 or $6?"
- **Comparison difficulty**: Hard to evaluate vs direct pricing
- **Rollover anxiety**: Fear of losing unused credits
- **Peak pricing confusion**: Variable credit requirements

#### User Complaints (from research)
- "I never know how many credits I need"
- "Credits expire before I can use them"
- "Math is too complicated"
- "Feels like airline miles - purposely confusing"

### 5. Vancouver-Specific Challenges

#### Geographic & Cultural Factors
- **Weather dependency**: 
  - 40% participation drop Nov-Feb
  - Outdoor classes frequently cancelled
  - Seasonal depression affects motivation

- **Cost of living pressure**:
  - Average rent: $2,500/month
  - Discretionary spending limited
  - Value consciousness high

- **Cultural diversity**:
  - Language barriers for some classes
  - Different comfort levels with group activities
  - Varying fitness/hobby traditions

#### Local Competition
- **Community centers**: $10 classes hard to compete with
- **Established studios**: Strong direct relationships
- **Free alternatives**: Beaches, parks, trails abundant
